## Why Salary Ranges Vary

### Key Factors
- **Yacht Size:** Strongest predictor of pay. Larger yachts = more complex systems, more guests, higher expectations
- **Role Technicality:** Engineers and ETOs consistently earn more (specialized knowledge)
- **Contract Type:** Rotational contracts pay 10-20% less than permanent but offer predictable time off
- **Regional/Currency:** Euro contracts fluctuate when converted to USD
- **Charter vs Private:** Charter yachts often include tips which can significantly increase total compensation

## Practical Scenarios (2025)

### Stewardess on 60m Yacht (Rotational)
- Base: $4,100 - $5,800
- Benefits: Valuable downtime, balanced career
- Trade-off: Slightly less than permanent but better work-life balance

### Captain of 180ft Superyacht
- Base: $16,000 - $19,000
- Experienced Masters: $25,000+
- With charter tips: Can rival top corporate executive packages

### Deckhand on 45m Private Yacht
- Base: $3,200 - $3,700
- No tips on private vessel
- May struggle to match charter yacht peers despite similar skill requirements

## Tax Implications (Critical for US Crew)

### US Citizens & Green Card Holders
- **IRS taxes worldwide income** - Cayman-flagged yacht salary is taxable
- Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE) may apply
- Tips must be declared - IRS has flagged non-reporting as audit red flag

### Example Tax Scenarios

**Stewardess earning $60,000/year:**
- Federal tax: $8,000 - $10,000
- Tips ($15,000): Must be reported or risk penalties

**Captain earning $200,000/year:**
- Federal tax: Could exceed $40,000
- Planning essential to minimize liability

### Foreign Crew
- French engineer working 2 months in US waters = US-source income subject to withholding

---

## Best Practices for Crew

1. **Compare offers fairly:** Match yacht size, ownership type, contract type
2. **Negotiate full package:** Flights, health insurance, training, repatriation
3. **Understand tip distribution:** Pooled vs equal vs weighted by rank
4. **Document everything:** Pay slips, contracts, tip records

---

## Key Takeaway

Salary benchmarks show what you should earn, but **after-tax planning determines what you actually keep**. The safest harbor is a smart paycheck: fair terms, tracked tips, and tax planning.
